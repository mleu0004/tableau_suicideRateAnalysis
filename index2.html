Suicide Victims from 1987 - 2016

Throughout the years, there has been countless news and articles relating to suicide cases, people taking their own life due to pressure or certain mental issues. This page right here focuses on how suicide cases progresses over the years, giving us an insight on certain factors related to the suicide cases

The visualisation here is showing how does the number of suicides per 100k progresses over time, specifically from 1987 to 2016.

From the visualisation, you can see that the point where the suicide cases is at its peak is at the year 1995. Until the year 2016year 2016, the number of suicides cases per 100k is reduced significantly to just around the 2000s.

The visualization on the left here is visualizing the suicidesPer100k for each age group. We see that the age group 75+ years has the highest suicides count per 100k, compared to the other age groups. However, for age group 5-14, the suicide count per 100k is almost 0, showing that kids age 5-14 have little to no suicidal thoughts.

Even with the above observations, the trend of suicides count per 100k for all these age group have a similar pattern, whereby their peak is at the year 1995, and decreases significantly at the year 2016.

This map of the world is a choropleth map, where the saturation of colours indicates the number of suicide cases per 100k for each country. The darker the color is, the higher the suicide rates in that country, and vice versa. Just glancing through the map, Russia seems to be most obvious country that has one of the highest suicide rates.

Countries in grey are excluded from the threshold of colors, due to the countries not having sufficent data regarding their suicide cases.

Top 10 Countries with Highest Suicide Rates

This bar graph is showing the top 10 countries with the highest suicide rates. By adjusting the slider below the graph, we can observe the top 10 countries for each year available. The pie chart here shows the proportion that each gender has occupied.

We can see that the male sex has significantly occupied the majority of the suicide cases with a stunning 76.89%, while the female sex occupied only 23.11%.
